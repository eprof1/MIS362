Exercise 1-Goal-Build a High Tech City

Time: 3 minutes

Envision the creation of a high technology Smart Gigabit Community in the southeast Minnesota region.

The goals of this project are:

  • Sustainable housing, clothing, food
  • Sustainable manufacturing, agriculture, silviculture
  • Progressive K-12 and higher education system using Open Educational Resources
  • High speed internet to support advanced digital technologies
  • Renewable energy
  • Advanced transportation infrastructure including
    • Autonomous vehicles
    • Vehicle to vehicle communications
    • Vehicle to infrastructure communications
    • High speed rail
    • Unmanned Aerial Vehicles
    • Autonomous barges
    • Facilities to handle intermodal containers

Definitions:

  • Product Owner: Professor Paulson
  • Development Teams: 5 or 6 teams of 4 to 6 students
  • Customer representatives
  • Scrum product teams: Product Owner + Development Teams

Focus on the first sprint.

(50) 1. Which one project goal is of most interest to you?



(50) 2. Which project goal do you feel is the most difficult to achieve?



(50) 3. Is this the first time that you have participated in a SCRUM activity?

Exercise 3-User Story Mapping

Time: 5 minutes

Begin by making a list of who will be the users of this product, the Smart Gigabit Community.

Write the names of your users on large Post-It notes.  These will become the top layer of the story map.

(50) 5. Generate a list of three or more users, in order of importance.

Exercise 4-User Needs

Time: 15 minutes

Discuss with your team which user should be satisfied first.

(50) 6. Make a list of things that your most important user will need.
It may help to imagine a day in the life of this user, as a way to generate their needs and wants.
These will be the second level of the story map.

Do this for at least three of your users.

Exercise 5-Features to Address User Needs

Time: 7 minutes

Each team brainstorms and develops ideas or features that will address these user needs.

Write these features on Post-It notes.  They are the third tier of the story map.  Place them on the board.

(50) 7. Write below some of the most important features for one of the users.

Exercise 6-Develop Releases

Time: 20 minutes

Working with the Facilitator and all teams, focus on the list of features and develop of list of those that will add the most value for the least effort.

Using blue duct tape, place three long lanes on the story map to create three separate releases.

One way to classify the relesases is as follows (feel free to modify)

  • Release 1-Minimum Viable Product
  • Release 2-Higher quality of service
  • Release 3-Scale up

Activity:  all team members are invited to go to the story board and move the features as they feel would be best.

(50) 8. List the features in Release 1.

Exercise 7-Product Backlog Refinement

Time: 2 minutes

Two members of each team will go to the story board, and take each features' Post-It note and place it in one of the columns (1, 2, 3, 5, 9, 13, 20).
These columns represent the relative effort required to complete that feature.
All participants are not to talk during this time.
Participants are allowed to move any Post-It as they see fit.

The other team members need to observe and make sure that they agree with the classifications.


(50) 9. What did the observing team members notice about this sorting process?

Exercise 8-Add Details

Time: 18 minutes

Each team takes an item(Post-It note) from the backlog board, brings it to their table, clarifies open questions, and writes these details on the card.
Then the item is placed back on the board, and takes another item and repeats the process.
Note that just because a team adds details to an item does not mean that that team will later be working on that item.

This process continues until details are added to all the items or 20 minutes passes.

Be sure to take a picture of the backlog matrix


(50) 10. What did the observing team members notice about this sorting process?

Exercise 9-Prioritize Product Backlog

Time: 5 minutes

The Product Owner (Professor Paulson) will arrange and prioritize the Product Backlog items based on his perception of the business priorities.
Possible guidelines:
  • items that define the plan of the city
  • proper building of housing early on
  • later building of complex items like hotels, conference centers
  • still later work on shopping centers, dining


Prioritized Product Backlog


(50) 11. What suggestion do you have to improve this prioritzation process?
