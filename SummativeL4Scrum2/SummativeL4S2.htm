Learning Objectives

These exercises allow you to participate in a SCRUM.

After completing this assignment you will be able to:

  1. Reflect on the benefits of SCRUM
  2. Provide feedback on this SCRUM  activity
  3. More fully participate in future planning activities
Assignment Overview
The goal of this assignment is to participate in a SCRUM and sprint(s) to build a model Smart Gigabit Community.

You have developed a working knowledge of this material from Chapter 9 of the book and the previous planning exercise.
If you are having trouble there are several resources available, besides a Google Search.

Review the Lynda.com video:  Scrum: The Basics  (1h2m)


Envision the creation of a high technology Smart Gigabit Community in the southeast Minnesota region.

The goals of this project are:

  • Sustainable housing, clothing, food
  • Sustainable manufacturing, agriculture, silviculture
  • Progressive K-12 and higher education system using Open Educational Resources
  • High speed internet to support advanced digital technologies
  • Renewable energy
  • Advanced transportation infrastructure including
    • Autonomous vehicles
    • Vehicle to vehicle communications
    • Vehicle to infrastructure communications
    • High speed rail
    • Unmanned Aerial Vehicles
    • Autonomous barges
    • Facilities to handle intermodal containers
Each team must choose a scrum master who:
  • observes planning sessions
  • seeks ways to improve processes
  • responsible for calculating burn-down rates
  • does not participate in sprint planning
Exercise 1-Plan the Sprint

Time: 3 minutes

 Goal-Move the Product Backlog (Post-It notes) on the left side into the Sprint Backlog (blue matrix) on the right side.

Post-It notes are prioritized from left to right, top to bottom
Blue matrix is for sprint placeholders, one for each team (4 teams shown)

Guidelines:

  • Team members must coordinate the card movement with all other teams.
  • Must be accomplished in a maximum of three (3) minutes.
  • Team scrum masters do not participate in this process, they only observe.

 

Focus on the sprint planning.

(50) 1. Define 'forecast' in terms of this SCRUM activity.



(50) 2. Define 'commitment' in terms of this SCRUM activity.



(50) 3. Is this the first time that you have participated in a SCRUM activity?



All participants are asked to raise from zero (fist) to five fingers, with more fingers representing higher confidence in the just created Sprint plan.


If the majority of hands raised are three or less, then some cards need to be moved back to the Product Backlog.


Exercise 2-Build an Increment

Time: 8 minutes

Each team will spend the time working on their Sprint backlog items.

Professor Paulson, the Product Owner, will be on a business trip during this time period. 

(50) 4. List the major issues your team encountered during this sprint.




Exercise 3-Review the Increment

Time: 5 minutes

The Product Owner (Professor Paulson) will ask for input from the teams on the Smart Gigabit Community.

  • What do you see?
  • How do you like the product being built?
  • This is what I see...  Do you agree?
  • What is missing?
  • What is not aligned?
  • Imagine you are a user of these buildings, what do you think?
  • How comfortable will this Smart Gigabit Community be for our users?

(50) 5. Jot down your impressions of the review.


Now generate new items to be added to the Product Backlog, so that they can be used in the next Sprint Planning session.


Exercise 4-Retrospective of the Process

 Time: 20 minutes total

Team Retrospective

Time: 7 minutes

Each team gathers at their table for a standup meeting.

Each Team Scrum Master leads a discussion to find out:
  • What worked well?
  • What needs to be changed in the next sprint so that more items get 'done'?
  • How can flow be made more continuous so items get sequentially inspected, improved and deployed?
  • What else can be adjusted in the process?


(50) 6. Write down the most important thing learned by your team while discussing the above questions.

Scrum Masters Retrospective

Time: 3 minutes

The Scrum Masters gather to discuss and do a quick calculation on the board.
The chart shows the burn-down rate, and is used to determine how many more sprints are needed to work through the current Product Backlog.

Overall Retrospective

Time: 5 minutes

Each team shares one key learning from the Team Retrospective that addresses what you plan to do differently in the next Sprint.

(50) 7. Write below what your team will do differently in the next Sprint.

Final Reflection

Time: 5 minutes

Consider issues that affect the overall organization

  • How can team's coordination and collaboration be improved?
  • What could the Product Owner start, keep or stop doing?
  • How else can the system be improved?

Exercise 6-Debrief

Total time:  20 minutes

Time: 5 minutes

  • What was most helpful building the Smart Gigabit Community despite all the unknowns and constraints?
